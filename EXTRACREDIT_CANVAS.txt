Feature 2 — Visual-literacy structure: Each chart has a short reader-facing caption (white hat: honest decoding; black hat: "surface reading" of what a quick glance suggests). The write-ups then explain the rhetorical mechanisms. This pairs artifact + likely takeaway + critique, similar to misinformation-literacy pedagogy—not just two plots with disconnected text.

Feature 3 — Verifiable data: Values are served from public data/emissions.json traced to Our World in Data / OWID CO2 data, with links on the page. That makes the white-hat numbers auditable and the black-hat tricks easier to verify against the same source.
